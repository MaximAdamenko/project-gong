Project Gong

How many people were in the Zoom call —
and how many of them actually spoke?

Four independent teams, two senses (sound + sight), one combined estimate — shown live from a single command.

The challenge

One recording in. Two numbers out.

Total

How many participants

Count the distinct people on screen across the whole meeting.

Speaking

How many actually spoke

Cross-check two independent signals: their voice and their lips.

The trick: no single signal is trustworthy alone. Audio misses silent listeners; video mistakes a smile for speech. We combine them.

Team 1 — Sound

Speech Detection (VAD)

Find when anyone is talking — slice the audio into speech segments, drop the silence.

How

Extract 16 kHz mono audio with ffmpeg, then run Silero VAD (a neural voice-activity model). Benchmarked vs an energy detector and WebRTC VAD.

Why Silero

Finer, cleaner segment boundaries → better speaker embeddings for Team 2.

Team 2 — Sound

Speaker Diarization

Find who is talking — group speech segments by voice identity to count unique speakers.

1 · Voice fingerprint

For each segment, Resemblyzer produces a d-vector embedding — a fingerprint of that voice.

2 · Cluster the voices

Agglomerative clustering on cosine distance merges segments that sound like the same person.

threshold 0.55  →  higher = fewer speakers · lower = more speakers

On the test recording: 4 unique speakers across 153 segments. This is the audio-based speaking count.

Team 3 — Sight

Face Detection & Tile Tracking

Find how many people — detect every face, give each Zoom tile a stable identity.

Detect

YOLOv8-Face finds every face in each frame as a normalized bounding box.

Stabilize → "slots"

Face centers cluster into fixed tile slots, so each participant keeps one face_id. Transient/background faces are filtered out.

Scan across the whole video, not just the opening  →  someone whose tile is only sometimes visible is still counted.

Result on the test video: 7 participants — the authoritative total.

Team 4 — Sight

Lip-Movement Detection

Find who is speaking on camera — measure each tracked face's mouth motion over time.

Mouth Aspect Ratio (MAR)

MediaPipe lip landmarks give MAR = vertical lip gap ÷ mouth width — scale-invariant, works at any tile size.

Movement = speech

A talking mouth makes MAR fluctuate; high variance over a window → speaking. A still or smiling mouth stays flat.

Live robustness: hysteresis (separate on/off thresholds) stops brief smiles, nods, and jitter from flickering a silent person to "speaking".

Step 5 — Fusion

Combining the four signals

total  =  faces detected  (Team 3)

speaking  =  round( ( audio speakers + lip speakers ) / 2 )  → clamped to total

Sanity check 1

Speaking can never exceed total → capped, with a warning.

Sanity check 2

Below ~70% speaking raises a warning to re-check thresholds.

Averaging two independent estimators makes the result robust to either one being noisy.

One command · live UI

python run_all.py --sound

On screen

  • Green/gray face boxes — speaking vs silent, live
  • Side panel: per-person status + running totals
  • Original audio plays in sync; final estimate when ready

Built to be robust

  • Heavy audio analysis runs in an isolated process
  • (torch + MediaPipe in one process crashes — so they're split)
  • Plays through the whole video; clean exit, nothing left running

Detection paces to real time and skips frames if the CPU falls behind, so playback always reaches the end.

Summary

Two senses beat one.

  • Team 1 hears when someone talks
  • Team 2 hears who is talking
  • Team 3 sees how many people are there
  • Team 4 sees whose lips are moving
  • Step 5 fuses them into one trustworthy answer
